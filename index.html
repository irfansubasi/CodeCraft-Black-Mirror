Black Mirror is an anthology series that explores the dark and unsettling sides of modern society and advanced technology. Each episode presents a standalone story set in a near-future or alternate reality, focusing on themes such as artificial intelligence, virtual reality, social media, surveillance, and consciousness transfer. Through its dystopian, thought-provoking narratives, the series questions how technological progress can expose and amplify humanity’s deepest flaws and fears.

MOST POPULAR EPISODES

White Christmas

Three interconnected tales of technology run amok during the Christmas season are told by two men at a remote outpost in a frozen wilderness.

Hang the DJ

Paired up by a dating program that puts an expiration date on all relationships, Frank and Amy soon begin to question the system's logic.

Black Museum

A woman enters the Black Museum, where the proprietor tells his stories relating to the artifacts.

San Junipero

When Yorkie and Kelly visit San Junipero, a fun-loving beach town full of surf, sun and sex, their lives are changed totally.

The Entire History of You

In the near future, everyone has access to a memory implant that records everything they do, see and hear. You need never forget a face again - but is that always a good thing?

Shut Up and Dance

When withdrawn Kenny stumbles headlong into an online trap, he is quickly forced into an uneasy alliance with shifty Hector, both at the mercy of persons unknown.

Hated in the Nation

In near-future London, police detective Karin Parke and her tech-savvy sidekick Blue investigate a string of mysterious deaths with a sinister link to social media.

Nosedive

A woman desperate to boost her social media score hits the jackpot when she's invited to a swanky wedding, but the trip doesn't go as planned.

USS Callister

Capt. Robert Daly presides over his crew with wisdom and courage. But a new recruit will soon discover nothing on this spaceship is what it seems.

Common People

When a medical emergency leaves schoolteacher Amanda fighting for her life, her desperate husband Mike signs her up for Rivermind, a high-tech system that will keep her alive.

EDITOR'S PICK FAVORITE SCENE

The ending of “White Christmas” perfectly captures Black Mirror’s darkest themes. Joe’s digital copy is left trapped in the cabin, experiencing what feels like thousands of years of isolation within just minutes in real time, condemned to eternal torment. Meanwhile, Matt is released but permanently blocked by everyone in society; every person appears as a grey, faceless silhouette, leaving him completely cut off from all human connection. This finale reveals how technology can create fates even worse than death – erasing reality, identity, and any hope of belonging.
